    Jamie Hersch's Bio: Into Sports From Early Age

    The charming media personality was born 25 May 1987 to a musician father, James, and Candy Hersch. She is one-half of two siblings in the family and has a brother. Her bio reveals that she was exposed to sports especially hockey and baseball from her early childhood.

    Jamie went to Champlin Park High School to pursue her high school education. She earned her bachelors degree from the University of Southern California, where she graduated in Broadcast Journalism and Religion in 2009.

    Her Broadcasting Career That Started With Annenberg TV

    Jamie started her broadcasting career from Annenberg TV news in 2006, where she was involved in reporting, anchoring and producing the news for three years. She was a weekend sports anchor and reporter for WKOW-TV until 2013.

    Joining FOX Sports in 2013 was the milestone in her career, where she covered the baseball and hockey tournaments of Minnesota Twins, Wild, and Gopher Hockey. She worked as a studio host and sideline reporter for FOX Sports for more than two years.

    Jamie then added to her working experience with her second job as a football and hockey reporter with the Big Ten Network in September 2015. 

    As of 2018, she is involved in anchoring various sports shows in NHL and MLB network joining in 2015 and 2016 respectively. Besides these two programs, Jamie also presents NHL Tonight not a regular basis but occasionally and supplements by providing the analysis of games and interview of players.
