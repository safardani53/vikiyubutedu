    HERSEY’S has discontinued its once-loved toaster strudels, manufacturer Pillsbury has confirmed.

    The supermarket item once offered customers a quick and easy chocolate breakfast.

    Cake manufacturer Pillsbury previously teamed up with Hershey’s to create toaster-ready chocolate strudels.

    Sold in packs of six, the pastries offered customers a convenient, chocolate breakfast.

    Recently, one fanon Twitter got in touch with Pillsbury to ask why he had not seen the item at the supermarket for so long.

    “Bring back Hershey’s chocolate toaster strudels,” he demanded.

    Read about more discontinued items

    The customer complained that his wife gets “sad literally every time we go grocery shopping” because she can’t find them.

    But Pillsbury replied to confirm that the strudels have been permanently discontinued.

    “The Hershey's Chocolate Toaster Strudels have been discontinued nearly two years ago,” wrote the company.

    “We'll let our team know how much your wife loves them and would like to see them back again. We love hearing from our fans!”

    Most read in Money

    Toaster strudels weren’t the only collaboration between Hershey’s and Pillsbury, which have both been around since the 1800s.

    Pillsbury previously sold frozen ready-to-bake cookie dough so customers could make their own Hershey’s cookies and cream cookies at home.

    However, this product has also been discontinued.

    Customers looking for alternatives to the Pillsbury Hershey’s strudels may look to the bakery brand’s strawberry cream cheese strudels, which are still available.

    MORE DISCONTINUED HERSHEY'S

    But this Pillsbury collaboration isn’t the only discontinued Hershey’s product that fans are sad over.

    The chocolate manufacturer has also confirmed that it has “no plans” to bring back Reese’s Swoops.

    Although these bite-sized treats have not been available in grocery stores since 2006,  Hershey’s fans on social media have not forgotten about them.

    One on Twitter vowed to “buy 100 boxes right now” if they were reintroduced.

    Meanwhile, another “gobble-worthy” Hershey’s product was taken off the shelves in 2007. 

    And customers are still longing for the return of the s’mores bar.
