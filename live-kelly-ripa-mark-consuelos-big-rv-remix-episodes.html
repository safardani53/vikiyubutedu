    LIVE's Kelly Ripa and Mark Consuelos have teased new episodes of their show Big RV Remix on Hulu.

    Kelly has been missing as of late on Live as she was previously on assignment.

    But that hasn't kept Mark from holding down the fort on the morning show.

    He also had something to share on social media for fans to get hyped about.

    Mark took to Instagram to share a clip of Big RV Remix, a series he's a part of.

    He captioned his clip: "All 10 episodes of our new show Big RV Remix are now streaming on Hulu."

    The series follows hosts Big Boi and Janice Faison as they remodel RVs for a variety of clients.

    Similar to the classic series Pimp My Ride, Big RV Remix finds the pair revamping everything from motorhome sto sprinter vans.

    The show is a part of Milojo Productions, Kelly and Mark's New York-based production company.

    The company got its name from the first two letters of the couple's children combined into one: Michael, Lola, and Joaquin.

    WHAT THE KELL

    Meanwhile, Kelly promoted a new episode of her game show, Generation Gap, after taking a break from Live.

    This week, the 51-year-old missed several episodes of Live with Kelly and Mark, which she usually hosts every day with husband Mark Consuelos.

    And amid her absence, Kelly urged viewers to tune into the latest installment of Generation Gap on Thursday evening.

    She hosts the competition, which features children working with their grandparents to answer questions about pop culture.

    The presenter shared a snap of her son Joaquin, now 20, when he was a child with his grandfather, Mark's dad Saul.

    The duo posed with gray Gap boxers on their heads in the playful shot.

    Kelly captioned it: "Just a friendly TBT reminder that an all new Generation Gap is on ABC tonight at 8/7c.

    "(No this is not a Gap ad, but it really should be)."

    The TV anchor also shared several clips from the all-new episode on her Instagram Story.

    Kelly's appearance on Generation Gap on Thursday comes after she joked that she won't return to Live if she wins the lottery.

    RIPA OUT

    After talking about a night out in the New York heat, Kelly let the audience know that she "might not be here tomorrow" on Monday's show.

    "Listen, just so you know," she said as she picked up a piece of paper from the desk.

    "I probably won't be here tomorrow or forever because I'm going to hit the $900 million Powerball."

    The audience cheered as Mark chuckled.

    Kelly said her plan would be to move to the South of France while Mark stayed behind to work on the show with actress Jenny Mollen.

    "Nobody will even notice I'm gone. They'll just think I've had work done," Kelly joked.

    "It'll be amazing. I've got this whole thing figured out."

    She continued: "I'll miss you all, but thank you."

    Kelly has missed several episodes of Live in recent weeks.

    A-list hosts such as Andy Cohen, Anderson Cooper, and Maria Menounos have been filling in during her time off.

    Last Thursday, Kelly was temporarily replaced by Bravo star, Andy Cohen, as she took a break from the show.

    Mark was joined by one of his and Kelly's best friends, Andy, at the hosts' desk.

    He teased that Kelly is currently on assignment, which is why she had to miss the broadcast.
