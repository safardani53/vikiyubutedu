    The 7 Best Lip Plumpers of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

    Fuller lips have been the beauty standard for quite some time, with many people overlining with lip pencils or turning to filler for a more permanent plump. But if you want to avoid the doctors chair and achieve a voluptuous pout at home, then you may want to give lip plumpers a shot.

    29 Feb 2024, 00:00

    Fuller lips have been the beauty standard for quite some time, with many people overlining with lip pencils or turning to filler for a more permanent plump. But if you want to avoid the doctor’s chair and achieve a voluptuous pout at home, then you may want to give lip plumpers a shot.

    With so many plumping products on the market, it’s hard to know which product is right for you. “[The] best ingredients to look for are hyaluronic filling spheres and vitamin E, and [be sure to] avoid parabens and sulfates,” says celebrity makeup artist Tobi Henney, known for glamming stars like Megan Fox and Ashley Graham for the red carpet (plump lips included). 

    With lip plumper often comes a tingling sensation, a result of the plumping agents within the product reacting to your skin. Henney states that you “should feel a little, as that will mean the plumping effect is working.” However, this should never hurt or be uncomfortable. 

    From sheer and pigmented shades to glosses and balms, there is an overwhelming amount of options to choose from. That’s why we took to the PEOPLE Testing lab to see which product reigns as the best once and for all. We tested 27 popular lip plumpers to evaluate their feel, ingredients, plumping ability, hydration, and finish.

    Here are the best lip plumpers that PEOPLE Tested.

    Best Overall

    Too Faced Lip Injection Maximum Plump Lip Plumper

    Sephora

    Pros

    • This lip plumper gave us the longest-lasting pout (we rarely had to reapply).

    • We saw the most physical plumping effects with this product.

    • The formula was never sticky or clumpy on our lips.

    When a product is named “Lip Injection Maximum Plump Extra Strength Lip Plumper,” you know it’s going to put in the work. This gloss from Too Faced scored five out of five stars for its plumping ability when we tested it in the PEOPLE lab, a score that was unrivaled (except for one other product by the same brand). Right off the bat, this product significantly and visibly plumped our lips, making them look fuller than they had originally. On top of that, we got a much longer wear time out of this tube than the others. Available in five glossy shades, ranging from shimmery formulas to high-shine finishes, this plumper was the star of the show.

    We do want to warn you about the tingling sensation, however, as this plumper really does what it says. The formula is made with “max-plumping technology” that made our lips a bit sensitive. While you won’t feel that annoyingly sticky texture, you may feel the burning sensation that comes with it — so proceed with caution. 

    But despite having a potentially uncomfortable tingling formula, its hydrating properties quickly erase any irritation. We were left with soft lips after wearing the plumper for a prolonged period of time, which made our lips look even more supple than before. Living up to its name, we can confirm that this is the most intense plumper we’ve tried yet.

    Things to Consider Before Buying Lip Plumper

    Formula

    As it is with all beauty products, the formula is a huge factor to consider when buying new makeup. There are many ingredients to look out for when purchasing a lip plumper, such as hyaluronic filling spheres and vitamin E, according to Henney (which you can find in the e.l.f. Cosmetics Lip Plumping Gloss, the Tarte Maracuja Juicy Lip Plump, and more). Henney also recommends steering clear of sulfates, which are potentially irritating ingredients. Another formulaic aspect to consider is the consistency you are looking for — whether it be heavy plumping with the accompanying tingling sensation or a light pucker with a minty-clean feel. A lip plumper with sun protection is also a good addition to your summer SPF makeup.

    Hydration

    Though you may use lip plumpers just as a lip gloss, many use them to prep the lips as well for a fuller base. In this case, it’s vital to choose a moisturizing lip plumper that can nourish the lips while simultaneously providing that pout. While all our picks gave us lusciously moisturized pouts, we thought that Patrick Ta’s Major Volume Plumping Lip Gloss was the most hydrating lip plumper thanks to its formula of skin-loving peptides. Sticky formulas often lack moisturization, so be sure to select a plumper that feels weightless and smooth. Alternatively, you can also just go with a lightweight gloss that's completely moisturizing with a little plumping effect, such as Cool Gloss from Jones Road Beauty.

    Finish

    We all go for different makeup looks on a day-to-day basis, as different events call for different glam. However, the most common finishes you can find when it comes to lip plumpers are shimmer, glitter, and high shine (you can even find all three in the Too Faced Lip Injection Power Plumping Lip Gloss). The latter is ideal for everyday or formal events, whereas shimmer and glitter finishes are great for a night out or anything that warrants getting a little dressed up. But at the end of the day, how you want to wear lip plumper is how you should — just another factor to keep in mind as you shop!

    How We Tested Lip Plumpers

    To find out which lip plumpers were actually able to give us the fuller lips we were looking for, we went through rigorous testing over a series of months, both in and out of our lab. Aiding us in this quest was celebrity makeup artist Tobi Henney, who shared the wisdom she’s gained over years of celebrity makeup on the dos and don’ts when it comes to lip plumpers. 

    • Application: We began by researching and compiling 27 of the most popular lip plumpers on the market to test in our PEOPLE Tested lab. Once our products were in, it was time to get glammed. We came into the room with makeup and product-free lips and had our pictures taken for the textbook “before” images. Next, we applied the product of choice to our lips, observing how it felt and looked on our lips. We then waited for 15 minutes to let each plumper fully work its magic, taking stock of the progress we observed and evaluating the end result. This is when we snapped the “after” picture — making for a great side-by-side.
    • Hydration: After 30 minutes, we wiped the lip plumper off and noticed how hydrated our bare lips looked and felt. We rated each category, from overall feel to plumping abilities and hydration, on a scale from one to five (one being the lowest-performing rating and five being the highest) and left our thoughts and feelings on how each performed in the end.
    • Long-Term Testing: But we didn’t stop there — after our lab test was done, it was time to send the lip plumpers home with a new group to get another person’s opinion on how each product performs long-term. Over the course of six months, we checked in at the one-, three-, and six-month marks to share information about how each lip plumper continued to fare (or fell short in the process). We used these insights and updated each product with a “how it’s held up” section to rehash these feelings with you all so you know what you’re getting yourself into.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    • How does lip plumper work?

      Lip plumpers provide that pouty, pillowy look by way of mild irritants in the formula that deliver temporary results without actually causing inflammation to your skin. Jeanine Lobell, celebrity makeup artist and founder of Stila and Neen, calls out peppermint, cinnamon, and caffeine as a few natural ingredients that will provide that tingly pout. Lobell also recommends lip plumpers with hyaluronic acid and shea butter for hydration and peptides which she says increase existing collagen in the lips for added plumpness in the long run, too. While all of these ingredients are generally safe, people who are prone to allergies or have sensitive skin should exercise more caution when trying these products, as they are more susceptible to irritation. Avoiding these ingredients entirely, you could try a product like Gloss Drip from the popular Kylie Cosmetics line to achieve the look of plumper lips.

    • Can you use lip plumper every day?

      "All lip plumpers are safe to use several times a day, every day if you don’t have a reaction to the ingredients," says Lobell. Just like with lip oils, many plumpers also include moisturizing ingredients that lock in hydration, which can make them more comfortable for all-day wear. If you start to feel more irritation than a faint tingle, then we wouldn't recommend continual use — but if you don't, feel free to apply lip plumper as many days in a row as you'd like.

    • How long does lip plumper last?

      You can expect to get a maximum of two or three hours of effect from a plumping gloss. However, lip plumpers like the Patrick Ta gloss are so hydrating (and long-lasting) that they only require a few applications a day.

    • Does lip plumper damage your lips?

      A slight tingling sensation is normal with most of these glosses, but it should be mild and wear off after a few minutes — thus being a safe product for your lips. Lobell ensures us that as long as you do not have a reaction to the ingredients, then you will not experience skin damage from your new lip plumper.
