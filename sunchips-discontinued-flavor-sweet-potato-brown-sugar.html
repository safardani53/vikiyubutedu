    SUNCHIPS has discontinued a fan-favorite flavor.

    The major household snack brand, owned by PepsiCo, currently has 16 different chip options listed online.

    One fan asked about the sweet potato and brown sugar flavor that is missing from the list and was wondering if there were plans to bring it back this fall.

    The customer asked SunChips on X, formerly known as Twitter: "We miss them in our house and would love to see them again. Why were they discontinued?"

    In response, the chip brand said: "Unfortunately not all of our flavors make the favorites list, and that one is currently enjoying retirement."

    Meanwhile, SunChips did say that it would share the customer's feedback with its team.

    The flavor's ingredients consisted of a mix of whole corn and whole wheat.

    Some other ingredients included sugar, butter, salt, natural flavors, maltodextrin, honey, and seed oils.

    When in stock at Walmart, a seven-ounce bag sold for $8.81. That equals $1.26 per ounce.

    Customers online rated the product 4.4 stars out of five.

    "These are absolutely my favorite flavor of Sunchips," one five-star review read.

    "They taste amazing and are the best sweet potato chip flavor I've had."

    Another positive commenter said: "We love the taste of the sweet potato and brown sugar. It is different from other sweet potato chips."

    OTHER SUNCHIPS FLAVORS

    Currently, SunChips has several other flavors that are available for a limited time only online.

    This includes an entire organic lineup, which features the following varieties:

    • Chili lime
    • Zesty queso
    • White cheddar
    • Garden salsa
    • Sweet hickory smoke

    In addition, the Monterey Jack and garden flavor is available for a limited time only.

    SunChips' original, French onion, regular garden salsa, black beans southwestern queso, and black beans spicy jalapeno varieties are all in its regular lineup.

    Keep in mind the availability can vary by store.

    MORE DISCONTINUED ITEMS

    SunChips is far from the only brand that has been axing flavors from its lineup.

    Another includes Doritos, which has discontinued the hot mustard flavor.

    Along with mustard, the chips were filled with spices, sour cream, natural and artificial flavors, onion powder, Monosodium Glutamate (MSG), and seed oils.

    At Walmart, the mustard-flavored Doritos sold for $3.88 in a 9.25-ounce bag.

    But Doritos did leave the possibility that the variety could return in the future if enough fans beg for it to come back.

    Kellogg's Cheez-It recently confirmed that it has discontinued the sweet and salty snack mix.

    It featured popular M&M's milk chocolate pieces, salted pretzels, sugar corn squares, and caramel-flavored popcorn along with the Cheez-it original pieces.

    Some other ingredients in the box included artificial and natural flavors, seed oils, and artificial food colorings.

    Per serving, there are five grams of added sugar.

    In an eight-ounce box, the treat sold for $7.53 at Walmart.

    Pringles has discontinued its popular honey mustard flavor.

    The variety was last seen discounted online for $1.96 in a 5.5-ounce container at Walmart.

    When it comes to grocery brands, Wegmans has discontinued a popular soda.

    And Publix has stopped selling a popular cake.
