    19 Apr 2024, 00:00

    DUCK Dynasty's John Luke Robertson and his wife Mary Kate's sweet Louisiana home - which was hit by bullets in a drive-by shooting last Friday - has a country chic vibe.

    The reality star has decorated the home in a neutral and peaceful vibe - which were revealed on Instagram before the alleged incident.

    The couple - who share 6-month-old son John Shepherd - moved into their home in January 2019.

    Plenty of wood and cream fabrics keep the feel of the home bright and natural.

    Since welcoming their son, they also have a baby's room with crib set up.

    His books and cuddly toys are on display as the sleeping area is set up next to patio doors.

    The couple have been married for five years, after first meeting at a summer camp when they were both 15.

    John Luke and Mary Kate and their baby live on the family compound in West Monroe, Louisiana, with dad Willie and mom Korie and other Robertson family members.

    On Friday, The US Sun reported around "eight to 10" shots were fired at Willie's property on Friday, with two of of his homes hit on his West Monroe, Louisiana estate.

    Of the nearly dozen shots fired off, one went through the bedroom window where Willie's son John Luke Robertson, 24, lives with Mary Kate and their six-month-old son.

    At the time of the incident, which didn't result in any injuries, Will was out shopping.

    He later said: “We were pretty shook up.

    “It looks like they were just spraying bullets across my property.”

    The famous outdoorsman revealed that he believes the suspects knew it was his property they sprayed bullets at.

    "I'm 100% certain, but I don't know why," he said.

    Bullets hit the homes that are behind a secured gate and far away from the main road.

    "Nobody was outside at the time, but everybody had been out about 5 minutes before. I had just gone to the store when it happened," Willie added.

    The shooting suspect has been ordered to "stay away" from the property.

    Daniel Dean King Jr., the man accused of shooting at the TV star's Louisiana house, has also been told by authorities to surrender his firearms.
