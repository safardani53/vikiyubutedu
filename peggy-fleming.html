    Peggy Fleming | Early Life, Family, And Education

    Peggy Fleming was born on July 27, 1948, in San Jose, California, to newspaper journalists and former U.S. marine Albert Fleming and Doris Elizabeth Fleming.

    Albert inspired his little girl to skate and took all his daughters skating. After that, she began to slide professionally at the age of nine.

    She is the second oldest of four sisters. The former ABC commentator has an older sister named Janice Fleming and two younger sisters named Maxine and Kathy Fleming.

    Sadly, Peggy lost her younger sister and her father to heart disease. Her father passed away months before she won Olympic gold at age 17.

    The Olympian is very close to her family, especially her mother, Doris Elizabeth. However, she credits her father for helping her find her sport and passion.

    She also thanks her mother for giving her the support and inspiration needed to continue.

    One of her famous quotes regarding her success and her parents is: “I wouldn’t have started skating if it weren’t for my Dad, but I became a skater because of my mom.”

    Her first coach was William Kipp, who died tragically with the United States figure skating team in Sabena Flight 548 crash. Then, she was coached by Carlo Fassi.

    The former athlete was very good at her sport and won five U.S. titles before winning an Olympic gold medal.

    She attended Hollywood Professional School and graduated with excellent academic performance. After that, she joined Colorado College to achieve a degree.

    Peggy Fleming | Skating And Other Career

    Figure Skating Career

    Peggy was only nine when she dreamed of becoming World Champion and started working towards her skating dreams.

    Her parents were her most powerful support system and the reason for her dream come true. Although she is not from a sports family, athletic energy is in her blood.

    Her dedication and hard work are truly reflected in her performance. After her coach’s tragic passing in a plane crash, she was coached by Carlo Fassi.

    Along with the coach’s excellency and hard work, the then-teenage fleming won five U.S. titles consecutively from 1964 to 1968.

    Also, in 1968, Fleming was the only American athlete to win a gold medal in the Olympics held at Grenoble, France.

    Moreover, she also won the World Championship title for three consecutive years, from 1966 to 1968. She officially retired in 1968 at the age of 20.

    Nevertheless, she did not quit skating. The former athlete is widely known as “the ice fairy” for her angelic performance on the ice.

    According to the non-profit news agency, The Associated Press’s 1993 edition, she was one of the most famous figure skaters, just behind gymnast Mary Lou Retton and figured skater Dorothy Hamill.

    Career In Other Fields

    After her Olympic win, Peggy gave numerous performances on television shows. The “ice fairy” appeared in five NBC specials.

    Moreover, she toured with several well-known skaters in skating shows such as Ice Capades. Besides that, she filmed a T.V. show in Russia and skated in China during the Cold War.

    As a result of great appreciation from fans, she was offered to be a commentator for ABC. Sports. After accepting the job, she was always in the spotlight and a familiar face in the athletic business.

    Notwithstanding that, she is famous for her commentating and analytical skills. Besides figure skating, she has explored many other fields in her life.

    After winning her battle against cancer, she became a breast cancer activist and encouraged women to discuss it and vouch for early detection.

    She also owned a winery along with her husband. The winery located in California was called Fleming Jenkins Vineyards & Winery.

    They produced and distributed almost 2,000 cases of “Choreography” and “Victories Rosé.” 

    The majority of the earnings from Victories Rosé supported charities and organizations that researched and raised awareness about breast cancer.

    Eventually, the winery closed in 2011. Apart from that, Peggy also starred in a movie named Blades of Glory.

    Peggy Fleming | Trophies

    Fleming has an award named after her called the Peggy Fleming Award. The trophy is given to skaters with excellent skills.

    Peggy awards the trophy in collaboration with the Broadmoor Skating Club. 2018 was the trophy’s inaugural year. It has had four sessions, and the 2021 Peggy Fleming trophy was awarded to Karen Chen.

    Tomoki Hiwatashi of DuPage FSC wins the fifth season of the Peggy Flaming award. She received the prize money of $3000. 

    Achievements 

    Fleming received a gold medal at United Figure Skating Association (USFSA) Bay Area Juvenile Championship in 1959.

    She then won three gold medals at USFSA Pacific Coast Juvenile Championship in 1960, 1961, and 1962.

    Peggy also won a silver medal in 1962 at the USFSA National Novice Championship. She then won a bronze medal at USFSA National Junior Championship in 1963.

    The Olympian was placed sixth and seventh at the Innsbruck Winter Olympic Games and International Skating Union (ISU) World Championship in 1964.

    Similarly, flaming won five gold medals at the USFSA National Championship from 1964 to 1968. Fleming also won three gold medals at the ISU World Championship from 1966 to 1968.

    She also won a bronze medal at the ISU World Championship in 1965. Likewise, she was named ABC Athlete of the Year in 1967.

    Fleming received the Babe Didrickson Zaharias Award in 1988. She also won a gold medal for women’s figure skating in Grenoble Winter Olympic Games in the same year.

    The former figure skate was inducted into the U.S. Olympic Hall of Fame in 1974, the USFSA Hall of Fame and World Figure Skating Hall of Fame in 1976, and the International Women’s Sports Hall in 1981.

    Moreover, she was awarded U.S. Olympic – Committee Olympic Spirit Award in 1967.
