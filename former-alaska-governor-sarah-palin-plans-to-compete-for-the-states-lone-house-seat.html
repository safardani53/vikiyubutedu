    Former Alaska Governor Sarah Palin Plans To Compete For The States Lone House Seat

    Sarah Palin, the former governor of Alaska, is planning to compete for the states lone congressional seat. After the death of the Republican Partys longest-serving Congressman, Don Young, last month, the seat became vacant.Palin announced her candidacy on Twitter, saying she would be pleased to serve the people of Alaska. Her tweet stated:

    11 Jun 2024, 00:00

    Sarah Palin, the former governor of Alaska, is planning to compete for the state’s lone congressional seat. After the death of the Republican Party’s longest-serving Congressman, Don Young, last month, the seat became vacant. Palin announced her candidacy on Twitter, saying she would be pleased to serve the people of Alaska. Her tweet stated:

    Sarah Palin said in her announcement that she is aware of the obligations of her candidacy and is prepared to honor Representative Young with her efforts. She said,

    “I recognize that I have extremely large shoes to fill, but I want to honor Representative Young’s legacy by sacrificing myself in the name of service to the state he loved and fought for, because I share his passion for Alaska and the United States of America.”

    The former Republican Vice Presidential candidate went on to attack her opponents and address topics such as “out-of-control inflation,” “empty shelves,” and high “gas costs.”

    She said, ”

    “America has reached a tipping point.” As I saw the extreme left devastate the nation, I felt I had to take a stand and join the battle… We need energy security in this nation, and Alaska can help give it – but only if the federal government gets out of the way and allows the free market to do its thing.”

    “I’m in this race to win it and join the struggle for liberty with other patriots prepared to give everything to defend our nation.”

    Sarah Palin’s decision to run for Congress signals her comeback to politics more than a decade after resigning as Governor of Alaska in 2009.

    A Look at Sarah Palin’s political career and departure.

    Sarah Palin was initially elected to the Wasilla City Council in 1992, and she was elected mayor in 1996. In 1999, she stood for re-election and beat incumbent John Stein for the second time. Palin was elected President of the Alaska Conference of Mayors in 1999. Palin was named head of the Alaska Oil and Gas Conservation Commission after an unsuccessful race for lieutenant governor in 2003. Between 2003 through June 2005, the politician was one of three directors of Ted Stevens Excellence in Public Service, Inc., a non-profit organization that offers political training to Republican women in Alaska.

    Palin was elected Governor of Alaska in 2006, becoming the state’s youngest governor and the first woman to hold the role. She rose to prominence in politics after serving as Republican presidential contender John McCain’s running mate in 2008. However, the two were defeated by Obama-Biden in November of that year, and Sarah Palin resigned as Governor of Alaska in 2009. Palin told ABC News a few months after she resigned that she was taken aback by the media’s reaction to her choice.

    She said that she did not anticipate her departure to be a “big issue,” and she could not understand why the general public was “confused” about the matter. She said,

    “Do you know why they’re perplexed?” I think people can’t accept anything at face value these days.”

    Palin also said that “legal costs” were a big factor in her decision to leave the government in the aftermath of suspected ethical allegations leveled by her opponents. She said, ”

    “You know, things in Alaska’s political arena have truly shifted since August 29, when I was nominated to run for Vice President.”  When the opposition research began – those researchers really assaulted Alaska – they began digging for dirt and have not stopped. They aren’t going to discover any dirt.”


    The former Alaska Governor also discussed escalating personal and state spending. She continued,

    “We keep showing that every time we win an ethical violation litigation, and we’ve won them all.” However, it has cost our state millions of money. Todd and I have paid for it. You know the enemy would love to see us declared bankrupt so that we can’t afford to flee.”

    Sarah Palin also stated that the prospect of being a “lame duck” after announcing her intention not to pursue a second term as governor pushed her to leave.

According to reports, over 40 candidates have already applied for the open special election. The extraordinary primary election is set for June 11.
