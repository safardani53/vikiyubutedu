    Konstantin Yevgeniyevich Koltsov is a Belarusian ice hockey sensation. A former NHL player, Koltsov last played for HC Dinamo Minsk in the KHL.

    Notably, he has left an indelible mark on the sport with his remarkable career spanning over 18 seasons.

    Known for his incredible speed, and stickhandling, Koltsov’s journey encompasses various leagues and teams.

    Koltsov’s journey to professional hockey began in his homeland, Belarus. He primarily showcased his skills playing for Junactva Minsk in the Belarusian 1st division.

    Nicknamed the “Russian Rocket II”, Koltsov was selected by the Pittsburgh Penguins as the 18th overall pick in the 1999 NHL Entry Draft.

    Similarly, his tenure with the Wilkes-Barre/Scranton Penguins in the AHL provided him with invaluable experience.

    Following the NHL lockout, Koltsov ventured into the European circuit and showcased his talents in the Russian and Kontinental League.

    He won the Russian Super League, Kubok Spartaka, Kubok Prezidenta, Kubok Romazana, and Kubok Polesya in his illustrious career.
