    WALES have called up Cardiff Blues trio James Botham, Lloyd Williams and Willis Halaholo after an injury crisis hit their squad.

    Wayne Pivac's side head to Scotland to face Gregor Townsend's firing Scotland team in the second round of Six Nations fixtures on Saturday.

    Dan Lydiate has been ruled out for the season with an anterior cruciate ligament injury picked up after just 10 minutes of action in the 21-16 win over Ireland.

    Flanker Botham, 22, the grandson of England cricket legend Sir Ian Botham, has been introduced to the squad under Pivac and already has three caps.

    The WRU confirmed Lydiate, 33, is out long term after injuring his knee at the Principality Stadium on Saturday.

    They said in a statement: "He will undergo a number of assessments and consultations in the coming days to establish the best course of management."

    Lydiate said on Instagram: "Well what can I say, been over two years since I have had the chance and opportunity to be involved with the @welshrugbyunion... even though it was short lived really enjoyed my time in camp with the players, coaches and staff."

    Scrum half Tomos Williams has picked up a hamstring injury and is replaced by Lloyd Williams, meaning there is no return for Rhys Webb.

    New Zealand-born centre Halaholo is uncapped and joined the Cardiff Blues in 2016, meaning he qualifies via residency.

    He was picked by Pivac in 2019 but had to withdraw through injury before the squad even assembled.
