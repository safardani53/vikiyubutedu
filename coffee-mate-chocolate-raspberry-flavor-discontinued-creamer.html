    CUSTOMERS are begging Coffee-Mate to bring back this unique discontinued creamer flavor.

    The brand used to make a chocolate and raspberry version of the product.

    "Can you bring back the white chocolate raspberry creamer?" one Twitter user asked.

    "Love it so much, miss it even more."

    But Coffee-Mate's corporate account replied that there are no plans to bring the product back.

    "While that flavor is discontinued, we can certainly make note that you miss it!" the tweet says.

    READ MORE MONEY

    It's not clear exactly when the product was axed, but it happened sometime before 2012.

    That's when a Facebook page dedicated to bringing the creamer back was created.

    "I'm not buying anymore Nestle products until they bring back Chocolate Raspberry Creamer," the page's creator said in one post.

    "I'm not the only one who loved this coffee creamer," the creator said in the last update added to the page in 2015.

    Most read in Money

    "Nestlé pay attention. This was always sold out before any other flavor."

    The post has been liked by 359 people and shared 30 times.

    "This was my favorite creamer," one commenter said.

    "I want it back badly," said another.

    For a different Facebook user, the product brought back nostalgic memories.

    "This was always my favorite from the time I was a kid," they said.

    "I’d get into my moms and drink it, because I wasn’t allowed to have coffee yet. They need to bring this back!!"

    But another commenter didn't use the creamer in their coffee.

    "We put it in Dr. Pepper, a great combo," they said.

    "I pour it on ice cream," a different user replied.

    They suggested trying the creamer "with Coca Cola or root beer or Pepsi."

    The Coffee-Mate version of this creamer may be discontinued, but another brand still sells a very similar product.

    International Delight has a white chocolate raspberry creamer that's still available at stores like Walmart, Safeway, and Meijer.

    Coffee-Mate's parent company Nestle did not immediately respond to The U.S. Sun's request for comment.
