    Hideki Tojo was a Japanese politician and shogun in the Imperial Japanese Army, who served as Prime Minister of Japan and served as president of the Imperial Government Aid Empire Association for much of World War II. During his years in power, he held a number of other positions, including the position of Chief of Staff of the Royal Army before finally being dismissed in July 1944. Hideki Tojo was born December 30, 1884 in a low-level samurai family. in Tokyo's Kōjimachi district. He began his career in the army in 1905 and gradually rose through the ranks to become a general in 1934. On March 1, 1937, he was promoted to Chief of Staff of the Kwantung Army, thanks to which he commanded military campaigns against the Chinese in Inner Mongolia. and the provinces of Chahar-Suiyan. In July 1940, he was appointed Minister of War in the Japanese government headed by Prime Minister Fumimaro Konoe.
