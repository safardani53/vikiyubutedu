    SEVERAL scheduled comic performances have been canceled, and fans think they know why.

    Pete Davidson, 29, announced that a selection of his shows won't go ahead, and some of his fans quickly noticed a link to his upcoming court date.

    Stand Up Live, whom Pete was slated to perform for, sent out an announcement about his shows.

    The organizer wrote: "We're really sorry but we just heard that Pete has to cancel his shows at Stand Up Live from July 27th-July 30th due to scheduling conflicts," and offered full refunds.

    One fan of the Saturday Night Live alum reposted the note to Reddit and added: "Phoenix dates July 27-30 canceled.

    "I have seen some ppl wondering why all the cancellations and I am just assuming but I do believe it has something to do with the crash.

    The social media user continued: "He would have had many lawyers appointments, court appearances.

    "The one in Phoenix I knew would be canceled since his court date is July 27th and I’m sure he would need to appear in California court."

    The fan concluded: "I think people need to give the man some leeway. I don’t believe he canceled all these shows for fun."

    Another fan of the TV and film star claimed in response: "His friend Dave responded to someone on Twitter who was asking if Pete was ok and about the shows he said they will be rescheduled."

    They added: "My guess is he’s gonna wait till the outcome of his charge... and then reschedule them [because] I guess he can’t do that till he knows what’s what."

    STARS COLLIDE

    Back in March, Pete and his girlfriend Chase Sui-Wonders, 27, were involved in a frightening car accident.

    The Suicide Squad actor was reportedly behind the wheel of the Mercedes car, as first reported by TMZ.

    Allegedly, Pete was speeding when he lost control just before midnight.

    During the car ride, the vehicle hopped over a curb and ran over a fire hydrant.

    The vehicle eventually slammed into the side of a nearby house in Los Angeles.

    'DEVASTATING'

    The Los Angeles County District Attorney's Office charged the comedian with one count of reckless driving, according to TMZ.

    A spokesperson for the DA's office told the outlet: "Luckily, no one was seriously injured as a result of this collision.

    "We know that reckless driving can have devastating consequences. In 2022, traffic fatalities in Los Angeles have reached the highest levels seen in 20 years.

    "This is an alarming trend that we cannot ignore. As a result, it's crucial that we take all allegations of reckless driving seriously and hold those responsible accountable."
