    Firefighters battle blaze at Stratford tower block close to London's Olympic Village

    DOZENS of firefighters were battling a blaze at tower block close to Londons Olympic Village early this morning. Witnesses said they saw residents screaming and running from the burning building on Leather Gardens in Stratford, east London, at about 1.46am.

    11 Apr 2024, 00:00

    DOZENS of firefighters were battling a blaze at tower block close to London’s Olympic Village early this morning.

    Witnesses said they saw residents screaming and running from the burning building on Leather Gardens in Stratford, east London, at about 1.46am.

    Eight fire engines, 58 firefighters and police officers rushed to deal with the fire which started in a 17th floor flat.

    The London Fire Brigade said: “We were called at 01:22. Fire engines from Stratford, Plaistow, Leyton, East Ham, Bethnal Green and surrounding fire stations are at the scene.

    “The cause of the fire is not known.”

    The fire service confirmed that no one was injured in the blaze and around 100 people had left the building before crews had arrived at the scene.

    It received 26 emergency calls to 999 and confirmed the blaze was under control by 2.52am.

    The cause of the fire is under investigation.

    It comes less than a year after the blaze at Grenfell Tower in West London which killed 71.
