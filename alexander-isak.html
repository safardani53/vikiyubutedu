    Alexander Isak | Early Life & Family

    Alexander Isak, a Swedish soccer player, was born on September 21, 1999. The player was born to Mr. and Mrs. Isak in the Swedish municipality of Solna. Isak’s father’s name is Teame, whereas his mother’s name is unknown. 

    The player also has a brother named Sened Teame, both of whom were raised in Solna. Both brothers spent their formative years in Sweden’s Stockholm metropolitan area.

    Alexander grew up in a middle-class family with a strong East African migrant culture that still permeates his home.

    The parents of Alexander Isak are originally from Eritrea, a nation in the Horn of Africa that Ethiopia surrounds in the south, Djibouti in the southeast, and Sudan in the west.

    Alexander Isak’s parents decided to leave the country out of fear for their lives and to provide a better future for their unborn children as a result of the political scenario and the absence of human rights in the nation.

    They found Sweden as an ideal location where baby Isak was later born. Despite coming from a migrant family and growing up in Solna Municipality, his parents were able to pay for him to have an early education.

    The player decided to focus all of his energy on soccer, a sport he fell in love with, after observing how slowly migrant children progressed through school and how many of them dropped out at alarmingly high rates.

    This progress started to pay off immediately because he was soon invited to trials with the neighborhood team of his municipality, AIK Fotboll.

    Alexander Isak | Career 

    Advancement At AIK

    Alexander debuted for AIK’s first squad on February 28, 2016, when he came on as a substitute during a Swedish Cup away game against fourth-tier outfit Tenhults IF. 

    At 16 years and 199 days, Alexander became the Allsvenskan’s youngest goal scorer for AIK. He achieved the feat by scoring the second goal in his team’s 2-0 victory, securing the win for his team.

    Likewise, he scored his first goal at AIK’s home field, Friends Arena, on April 25 to help his team defeat IF Elfsborg 2-1.

    Alexander signed his first professional contract on May 3, 2016. The contract lasted through the end of the 2018 campaign.

    Isak’s two pivotal goals helped AIK defeat Djurgrdens IF 3-0 in the derby on September 21, 2016, his 17th birthday. He was then referred to as “Sweden’s next Zlatan Ibrahimović” by teammate Chinedu Obasi.

    Borussia Dortmund

    The player signed a long-term contract with Bundesliga club Borussia Dortmund on January 23, 2017, that ran through the summer of 2022.

    Although the transfer fee was not made public, rumors claimed it was €9 million ($9.25 million), the biggest sum ever paid for an Allsvenskan player. Also, Isak reportedly turned down a move to Real Madrid before signing with Dortmund.

    On March 14, 2017, he made his debut for the team in a 3-0 cup victory over Sportfreunde Lotte.

    Similarly, he won his first trophy with the team when Dortmund won the DFB-Pokal in 2016–17 despite making just one appearance and not making the final roster. 

    Playing For The Dutch Club

    Alexander was loaned to Dutch Eredivisie club Willem II after not making an appearance for Borussia Dortmund during the 2018–19 Bundesliga season.

    In a 3-2 victory over Fortuna Sittard on March 30, 2019, Isak made history by being the first player ever to score three penalties in an Eredivisie game.

    Further, on April 14, 2019, Alexander made league history by becoming the first player of foreign birth to score 12 goals in his first 12 league games.

    Real Sociedad

    Isak agreed to a five-year contract with Real Sociedad on June 12th for £988,000 ($1.16 million) per year. The player joined the La Liga team on July 1st and secured the first goal in his first game.

    Isak played well the rest of the preseason, tallying four goals in five contests.

    Similarly, he scored two goals and provided one assist against Real Madrid in the Copa del Rey quarterfinal on February 6.

    He also scored the game-winning goal against Athletic Bilbao in the Basque derby on February 9. 

    Isak won his first trophy with the team on April 3, 2021, as he started and participated for 89 minutes of Real Sociedad’s 1-0 victory over Athletic Bilbao in the 2020 Copa del Rey final.

    Further, he became the first Swedish player to score a La Liga hat-trick since Henry “Garvis” Carlsson.

    Alexander Isak | Transfer

    Newcastle recently acquired Alexander Isak on 26th August 2022 for an estimated £58 million ($68 million).

    Isak, 30, signed a “long-term” contract with the Premier League team.

    The player is Newcastle’s fourth acquisition since the conclusion of the previous campaign, joining Matt Targett, Nick Pope, and Sven Botman.

    Alexander Isak | International Career

    Alexander competed for the Swedish youth national team from U-16 to U-21. He was selected for the Sweden national team to play against Slovakia and Ivory Coast in friendlies on January 8 and 12, respectively.

    Likewise, in the 62nd minute of a 1-2 defeat, he made his international debut against the Ivory Coast, replacing Per Frick.

    He started the second game against Slovakia, scoring the opening goal in a 6-0 victory in the 19th minute. With this, the player set a Swedish national team record for the youngest goal scorer ever. 

    Alexander debuted for Sweden on March 23, 2019, when he came on as a substitute for Robin Quaison in the 88th minute of a 2-1 victory over Romania in a UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying match.
